The derivative of $f(x) = e^x$ is $f'(x) = e^x$. \newline\\

The function $f(x) = e^x$ \emph{is its own derivative}. \\[1cm]

\begin{example}
Find the derivative of each.
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
    \item $y = 7e^x$
    \item $y = x^4 \cdot e^x$
    \item $g(x) = \frac{x^2}{e^x}$
    \item $f(x) = \ln\left(e^x\right)$
\end{enumerate}
\end{example}

\texttt{Chain Rule for the Exponential Function}

If $f(x)$ is differentiable, then the derivative of $h(x) = e^{f(x)}$ is $h'(x) = e^{f(x)} \cdot f'(x)$

\begin{example}
Find $\frac{dy}{dx}$ for each.
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
    \item $y = e^{3x-3}$
    \item $y = e^{6x-0.5x^6}$
    \item $g(x) = e^{1-\ln(x)}$
    \item $f(x) = \ln\left(e^{3x}-8\right)$
\end{enumerate}
\end{example}

\subsection*{Applications}

The world's population during the 20th century closely follows the equation
\[
p(t) = 1.419e^{0.014t}  \quad [0, 100]
\]
where $t$ represents the number of years since 1900 and $p(t)$ represents the world's population (in billions). \newline\\

Evaluate and interpret $p'(10)$ and compare it to $p'(99)$.
